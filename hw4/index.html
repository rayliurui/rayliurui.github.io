Criticise the Visuals: Chinese Candidates for Oscar

Chinese films have been fighting for Oscar for 40 years with few significant achievement. Is the overall quality weak? Or are there other factors?

Dydata, a Chinese data visualization service platform, analyzes the films chosen as Chinese candidates for Oscar. The finding is that China's selection criteria are not based on good reputation.

Douban score is an important criterion for good films in Mainland China, which is generated by viewers themselves.

Four candidates scored under 6 so far, including House of Flying Dagders (2004), The Promise (2005), Curse of The Golden Flower (2006) and Xuan Zang (2016). Some films with higher scores didn't get the chance to chase for Oscar.

There are three variables: candidate scores, film scores in the same year and film number. However the chart is very confusing. It's easy to define the column as number rather than every unit at first sight. Readers will find it hard to understand the chart without annotation.

Also, the designer uses candidate score as the baseline to empahsize higher scores. But minus on y-axis complicates the interpretion.

The key element is the number because the designer doesn't show us exact score of each film. Therefore I think a stacked column chart is enough to tell readers "China's selection criteria for Oscar candidates are not based on good reputation".
